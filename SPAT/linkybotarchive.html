Linkybot Archive

Conceived of by WoC and coded jointly by him and virtuNat, Linkybot is a more simplistic bot function that simply spits out any message I've sent in the HSD over the last several years. These are usually considered funny if the output just happens to coincide with the query in some way. As I'm going through and actually adding a lot of these, I think it proves to be a more valuable repository than I thought; it showcases some of our humor in situ, and may be of some interest to historians or just as a reminder of the kinds of things we used to find funny.

Both Linkybot and its younger brother Drewbot together have been used thousands of times, and so I have a threshold for what I'm willing to put in here. User Kaisheng21 filtered messages by whether they have 10 or more reactions, or a combination of other reactions, and I've combed through the resulting list to see what gets put in the archive (my choices of what make the cut are somewhat arbitrary, if you think I've missed something important feel free to reach out to me).

Some notes: I have omitted a great number of the results as time goes on because the question/response were too simple or a repeat of previous jokes; I had forgotten that a running gag was to try and get the bot to say something inadvertently offensive, such as making it look sexist; other favored responses are mentioning the user who made the prompt, or strings of responses that all seem to coherently further the conversation (we tend to call it sentient in such exchanges). WoC also inserted a random chance--1 in 10,000, if I remember correctly--for Linkybot to spit out an image edit he made of me, which actually appears in this list a few times.

The overall trend seems to have been an increase in usage from 2020 through 2021, then tapering off slightly after that, and then especially after Drewbot came into prominence. I would recommend that if you see more than one entry from the same day, click through to see the context and just see the bot usage for that day directly to get a better/more accurate experience, it usually indicates a frenzy where the bot was unusually good for the day.
